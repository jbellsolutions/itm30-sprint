# ITM30 Sprint — Founder Intake Questions (Genius Tap Call)

Work through these sections one at a time. This is a full brain dump. Summarize what you learn after each section before moving to the next.

---

## SECTION 1: The Idea + The Offer

1. Describe the idea in one sentence — what is it?
2. What problem does it solve? Who has that problem?
3. Have you tested this idea in any form yet — prototype, beta users, early customers? What happened?
4. What makes your approach different from anything that currently exists?
5. What is the unique mechanism — the specific thing only you do or have?
6. What is your dream outcome for a client who goes through your offer?

---

## SECTION 2: The Founder

7. What is your background — what have you done professionally, and how does it connect to this offer?
8. What's the story that makes you the right person to build this? (The "why you" answer)
9. What is your earned authority in this space — what have you actually done that others haven't?
10. What other offers, businesses, or projects have you run before?
11. What do you want this business to look like in 12 months?

---

## SECTION 3: Target Audience + Niche

12. Who is the ideal customer for this offer? Be specific — not "entrepreneurs" but the specific type.
13. What is their #1 pain point right now? What keeps them up at night?
14. What have they already tried that hasn't worked?
15. Where do they hang out — LinkedIn, Instagram, specific communities, events, podcasts?
16. Who are the top 5–10 people (influencers, thought leaders) your audience already follows?
17. What would make them say "I need this right now" rather than "maybe later"?

---

## SECTION 4: The Prototype (Day 7)

18. What is the simplest possible version of this offer that proves the concept?
19. What does "working" look like on Day 7 — what should the prototype be able to do?
20. What tools, platforms, or technology does it need to run?
21. Do you have any existing assets — content, audience, email list, social following?
22. What's the #1 constraint that could slow down the prototype build?

---

## SECTION 5: The Founding Circle

23. Who are the 10–20 ideal people for your founding circle — what's their profile?
24. Do you already have relationships with people who fit that profile? Roughly how many?
25. What's the offer to founding circle members — lifetime access? Founding discount? Revenue share?
26. What do you need from them in return — feedback? Testimonials? Referrals?
27. How do you want to invite them — personal email, LinkedIn DM, phone call?

---

## SECTION 6: GTM + Outreach

28. What's the best cold outreach channel for your audience — email, LinkedIn, or both?
29. Do you have (or can you get) a list of ideal prospects? What does that list look like?
30. What's the messaging angle that would make your target audience stop and pay attention?
31. Are there any podcast circuits, communities, or events where your audience concentrates?
32. Do you have any existing content, a podcast, or a newsletter? What's worked so far?

---

## SECTION 7: The Expert Series

33. Who would be your ideal Expert Series guests — what profile, what industry?
34. What's the authority topic your audience most wants to hear about?
35. Do you have any existing relationships with potential guests?
36. What platform makes most sense for the podcast/interview — audio, video, LinkedIn Live?

---

## SECTION 8: The AI VA

37. What tasks do you need an AI VA to handle day-to-day after the sprint?
38. What hours and timezone works for your VA?
39. How technical does your VA need to be?
40. Any specific tools they must know or be trained on?

---

## SECTION 9: Logistics

41. What is your target sprint start date?
42. What is your preferred communication channel during the sprint?
43. Are there any hard deadlines, launches, or events we need to build around?
44. What's your budget for tools, ads, and setup (the $2,000 budget — how do you want to allocate it)?
45. Anything else we should know before we start?
